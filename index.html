Battle of Lexington and Concord (1775)

The Battle of Lexington and Concord took place on April 19, 1775, in Massachusetts. The British army, led by General Thomas Gage, fought against the American colonial militia, led by Captain John Parker. The outcome of the battle was a loss for the British, as the colonial militia was able to push them back. This battle was important because it marked the beginning of the armed conflict between the American colonies and the British forces.



Battle of Bunker Hill

The Battle of Bunker Hill took place on June 17, 1775, near Boston, Massachusetts. The British army, led by General William Howe, faced off against the American colonial militia, led by Colonel William Prescott. The outcome of the battle was a British victory, but the American militia put up a strong fight and inflicted heavy casualties on the British forces. There were also people from other countries involved, such as the Polish military engineer Thaddeus Kosciuszko, who helped the American forces build fortifications. This battle was important because it showed that the American colonists were willing to stand up to the powerful British army, boosting their morale and determination to fight for their independence.

Siege of Boston (1775)

The Siege of Boston started on April 19, 1775, after the battles of Lexington and Concord. General George Washington and the American militia surrounded the city where the British army, led by General Thomas Gage, was stationed. The siege went on for almost a year until the British troops left on March 17, 1776. This event was important because it proved that the American forces could effectively surround and put pressure on the British soldiers. It showed that the Americans were determined to stand up against the British and fight for their beliefs.




Battle of Trenton (1776)

The Battle of Trenton happened on December 26, 1776, during the American Revolutionary War. General George Washington and his soldiers crossed the icy Delaware River to surprise the Hessian mercenaries, who were fighting for the British. Even though the American troops were facing tough winter weather and low spirits, they won a big victory by capturing more than 1,000 enemy soldiers. This win was a big moment in the war, lifting American morale and showing that they could overcome challenges. While there were no famous foreign people involved in the Battle of Trenton, the success of this bold military move showed Washington's smart planning and the determination of the American forces to gain their freedom.


Battle of Saratoga (1777)

The Battle of Saratoga took place in 1777 during the American Revolutionary War. American forces, led by General Horatio Gates and General Benedict Arnold, fought against the British army commanded by General John Burgoyne. The Americans won a significant victory, which is considered a turning point in the war. This battle boosted American morale and convinced France to openly support the American cause.

Siege of Yorktown (1781)

The Battle of Yorktown happened in 1781 during the American Revolutionary War. American troops, led by General George Washington and supported by French forces under General Rochambeau, fought against the British led by General Cornwallis. The Americans and French won a crucial victory, which led to the surrender of the British army. This battle marked the end of major fighting in the war and ultimately secured American independence. The Battle of Yorktown showcased the strong partnership between American and French forces in achieving a significant triumph.

Foreign Battle Supporters


Tadeusz Kościuszko

Tadeusz Kościuszko, a soldier from Poland, helped the Americans a lot during their fight for freedom in the Revolutionary War. He was really good at planning battles and building forts. People liked him because he believed in freedom and fairness for everyone. He became famous for making strong defenses at places like Saratoga and West Point, which helped the Americans win important battles. Other soldiers and leaders respected him a lot for his leadership and dedication to the American cause. Even though he was from Poland, he became a hero in America for being brave and smart in the war. People still remember and honor him in both the United States and Poland.


Casimir Pulaski

Casimir Pulaski was a brave soldier from Poland who came to America to help in the Revolutionary War. He was really good at leading soldiers in battles and fighting for freedom. Pulaski played a key role in training American troops and organizing cavalry units, which are soldiers on horseback. His skills and courage were important in helping the Americans win battles against the British. Pulaski became known as the "Father of the American Cavalry" for his contributions to the war effort. People in America remember him as a hero who fought for freedom and independence.
